Stash It — Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026  ·  Version 4.12

What Stash It does

Stash It is a browser extension that lets you drag images from any webpage into a floating tray and drag them back out again. Your stashed images sync across your open tabs in real time.

Data we collect

None. Stash It does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. No analytics, no telemetry, no tracking of any kind.

Local storage only

Images you stash are stored exclusively in your browser's local extension storage (chrome.storage.local). This data never leaves your device. It is not sent to any server — not to the developer, not to any third party.

Network requests

Stash It makes one type of outbound network request: when you drop an image URL onto the tray, the extension may attempt a fetch() to download that image for local storage. This request goes directly to the image's original host — the same server your browser would contact anyway to display the image. No data about you is included in this request beyond what a normal browser request contains (e.g. your IP address as seen by that server).

If the fetch fails (e.g. due to CORS restrictions), no data is stored and a toast notification informs you of the failure.

Permissions

Stash It requests the following permissions:

storage — to save and sync your stashed images and tray preferences (position, size, mode) locally in your browser.

<all_urls> — to inject the tray UI on every page you visit, so you can stash images from any site. This permission is not used to read, monitor, or transmit any content from the pages you visit.

Ko-fi donation link

The tray includes a one-time dismissible link to the developer's Ko-fi page (ko-fi.com/kanameshizu). Clicking it opens Ko-fi in a new tab. Stash It does not track whether you click it or donate. The link can be permanently dismissed by clicking ×, and your choice is stored locally.
